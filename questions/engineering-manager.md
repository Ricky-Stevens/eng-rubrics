# Engineering Manager - Interview Questions

## A - Performance Management & Team Development

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about a challenging situation developing or managing someone on your team-it could be a difficult conversation, managing underperformance, developing someone's career, or building trust. What happened and how did you handle it?

2. Can you tell us about a situation where building psychological safety, trust, or a strong team culture was essential? How did you approach it?

3. Tell us about someone on your team you've had a significant impact on. What did you do, and what was the outcome?

4. Can you tell us about a time you had to make a difficult decision about someone on your team? Walk us through your thinking.

5. Tell us about your approach to developing your team. Give us an example of how this worked out.

6. Can you share an example of when you received feedback from a team member that challenged you? How did you respond to it?

7. Tell us about a time you noticed potential in someone that others might have missed. What did you do?

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about managing someone's underperformance.

2. Tell us about a difficult conversation you had to have with someone.

3. Tell us about helping someone progress to a new level.

4. Tell us about a time you gave feedback that was hard to hear.

5. Tell us about a mistake you made in managing someone and how you corrected it.

6. Tell us about a time you supported someone through a personal or professional challenge.

### Guided & Specific

1. Please describe a conversation where you had to deliver a difficult message or the outcome was uncertain. What made it challenging, and how did you approach it?

2. Please walk us through a time you saw someone on your team grow significantly or progress to a new level. What role did you play, and how did you track their progress?

3. Can you share an example of a time you had to manage conflict or disagreement between team members? What was at stake, and how did you resolve it?

4. Please walk us through an example of someone who initially struggled but you helped develop into a strong performer. What was the turning point?

5. Can you tell us about a time you had to have a performance conversation with someone who was generally good but had a significant blind spot? How did you handle it?

6. Please describe a situation where you identified a gap in how your team worked together or communicated. What did you do to improve it?

7. Please walk us through a difficult decision you made about someone's role or position on the team. What factors did you consider, and what was the outcome?

8. Tell us about a time you had to help someone transition out of a role or the team. How did you handle it?

## B - Technical Direction & Architecture Input

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about a time you influenced a significant technical decision on your team or in your organisation. What was the situation, and how did you approach it?

2. Can you tell us about a technical challenge or architectural decision your team faced? Walk us through how you guided or contributed to it.

3. Tell us about your approach to staying technically engaged with your team without being hands-on. Give us an example.

4. Can you tell us about a time you had to balance a technical consideration against a business constraint? What was the situation?

5. Tell us about the systems or architecture your team owns. How do you stay current with them?

6. Can you tell us about a time you learned something significant from your engineers about a technical topic? How did that influence your leadership?

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about managing technical debt.

2. Tell us about a time you pushed back on a technical approach.

3. Tell us about challenging a technical decision from your team.

4. Tell us about establishing or improving technical standards on your team.

5. Tell us about escalating a technical risk to leadership.

6. Tell us about advocating for a longer-term technical investment.

7. Tell us about ensuring consistency across your team's technical work.

### Guided & Specific

1. Please describe a situation where you had to make a call on a technical trade-off (cost vs. performance, speed vs. quality, reliability vs. time-to-market, etc.). How did you reason about it?

2. Can you walk us through a time when you disagreed with a technical approach your team or colleagues wanted to take? What was at stake, and how did you handle it?

3. Can you share an example of a technical decision or initiative where reliability, scalability, or architectural correctness was critical? How did you guide or ensure it was done well?

4. Can you share an example of when a technical decision didn't go as planned? How did you handle it, and what did you learn?

5. Please tell us about a situation where you had to escalate or surface a technical risk to leadership. How did you present it?

6. Please describe a time you had to explain a technical constraint or limitation to non-technical stakeholders. How did you make it clear?

7. Can you tell us about a time you had to have a technical conversation that changed someone's mind or perspective? What was the situation?

8. Please walk us through a time you evaluated a major technical initiative or refactoring. What was your decision process?

## C - Delivery & Accountability

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about a project or programme your team owned where you faced competing priorities, dependencies, or challenges with delivery. How did you manage it?

2. Can you tell us about a time your team faced a delivery challenge or crisis? Walk us through what happened and how you responded.

3. Tell us about your approach to delivery planning and accountability on your team. Give us an example of how this worked out.

4. Can you tell us about a time you had to make a difficult decision or trade-off about what to deliver and when?

5. Tell us about a situation where you had to be transparent with stakeholders about delivery challenges or constraints.

6. Can you tell us about a time you used metrics or data to inform a delivery decision? What did you track?

7. Tell us about protecting your team from disruption or distraction while maintaining delivery.

8. Can you tell us about a time you had to learn from a delivery failure? What went wrong, and what did you change?

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about missing a commitment and how you responded.

2. Tell us about reducing scope or changing timelines with stakeholders.

3. Tell us about delivering under resource or time pressure.

4. Tell us about escalating a delivery problem or blocker.

5. Tell us about reprioritizing your team's work mid-stream.

### Guided & Specific

1. Please describe a time you had to negotiate scope or timeline with stakeholders because your team couldn't deliver everything. How did you approach it?

2. Please walk us through a time you had to shield your team from disruption or distraction to protect delivery. What was happening, and how did you manage it?

3. Please describe a situation where your team had to deliver something significant under time or resource constraints. How did you maintain quality and sustainability?

4. Can you share an example of where you identified a delivery risk early and took action to mitigate it? What was the outcome?

5. Can you share an example of where you had to communicate honestly with your team or stakeholders about constraints, delays, or uncertain delivery? How did you handle it?

6. Please tell us about a time you managed dependencies across teams to unblock work or hit a deadline. How did you handle it?

7. Please walk us through your approach to planning and estimation on your team. Give us a specific example of how this helped.

## D - Stakeholder Communication & Influence

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about a situation where you had to communicate or influence a decision with stakeholders outside your team-whether that's leadership, product, or another function. What was tricky about it, and how did you handle it?

2. Can you tell us about a time you had to advocate for your team's needs-resources, hiring, time, visibility, etc.? Walk us through how you made your case.

3. Tell us about a relationship with a key stakeholder that's important to your work. How did you build it?

4. Tell us about a complex situation where you had to represent your team's perspective to people with different priorities.

5. Can you tell us about a time you had a difficult conversation with a peer manager or another leader? What was at stake?

6. Tell us about a time you had to adjust your communication style for a particular audience or stakeholder.

7. Tell us about navigating competing priorities or pressures from multiple stakeholders.

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about advocating for your team's needs.

2. Tell us about managing conflicting expectations from stakeholders.

3. Tell us about communicating bad news to leadership.

4. Tell us about influencing a decision with stakeholders.

5. Tell us about building trust with a skeptical stakeholder.

6. Tell us about pushing back on a request without damaging the relationship.

7. Tell us about managing upward-keeping your leader informed effectively.

8. Tell us about disagreeing with leadership and how you handled it.

### Guided & Specific

1. Can you share an example of when you had to shift a stakeholder's perspective or get them to support something you believed in? What was your approach?

2. Please describe a time you had to explain a technical constraint or limitation to non-technical stakeholders. How did you make it clear?

3. Please describe a time you had to use data or evidence to influence a decision with stakeholders. What convinced them?

4. Can you tell us about a situation where you had to build alignment across multiple teams or functions on something important? What was your strategy?

5. Can you tell us about a time you had to communicate a strategic decision or vision to your team and stakeholders? How did you get buy-in?

6. Please describe a time you had to be transparent about something difficult (a mistake, uncertainty, constraints) with stakeholders. How did that go?

## E - Hiring & Team Composition

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about a time you built or shaped your team through hiring or a difficult personnel decision. What approach did you take, and what was the outcome?

2. Tell us about your approach to hiring or team building. Give us an example of how this worked out.

3. Can you tell us about a time you had to make a difficult decision about someone's place or future on the team?

4. Tell us about the composition of your current or recent team. How intentional were you in building it?

5. Can you tell us about a situation where you identified a gap in your team's composition or skills and took action to address it? Walk us through what you did.

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about exiting someone from your team.

2. Tell us about maintaining hiring standards under pressure.

3. Tell us about diversity or intentionality in building your team.

4. Tell us about hiring from within or developing someone internally.

5. Tell us about rebuilding or restructuring your team.

6. Tell us about succession planning or reducing single points of failure.

7. Tell us about a time you defined roles or levelling for your team.

### Guided & Specific

1. Please describe a time you had to make a difficult call on someone's fit for the team or role. What was the situation, and how did you handle it?

2. Can you walk us through a hiring process you led where you used structured assessment or rubrics to evaluate candidates rather than relying on intuition? What was your approach?

3. Please tell us about a time you had to say no to a candidate or hire that looked good on paper but didn't feel like the right fit. What was your reasoning?

4. Please describe a situation where you deliberately built diversity or different perspectives into your team. What was important about that?

5. Can you tell us about a time you identified a future gap in your team's skills or capacity and planned ahead to address it? What did you do?

6. Please tell us about a situation where you had to rebuild or restructure your team. What prompted it, and how did you approach it?

7. Can you tell us about a time you maintained high hiring standards even when there was pressure to hire quickly or lower the bar? What happened?

8. Please walk us through your approach to succession planning or reducing single points of failure on your team. Give us a specific example.

9. Tell us about a time you had to make a difficult decision between two candidates or hires. What informed your decision?

## Interview Styles

### Open & Conversational
Broad opening that lets the candidate choose their own example. Minimal scaffolding or direction - they pick the situation that matters to them.

*Best for:* Natural flow conversations, discovering what they naturally value and emphasise, seeing their own priorities.

### Concise & Direct
Minimal preamble, straight to the point. Questions are tight and assume the candidate understands what you're asking for.

*Best for:* Efficient interviews, fast-paced discussions, candidates with strong self-direction, interviewer who prefers not to scaffold.

### Guided & Specific
Points toward a particular situation type, but lets them choose their own example. Scaffolded enough to be clear what ground you want to cover.

*Best for:* Ensuring specific competencies are explored, guiding discussion while respecting their choice of example, structured assessment.
