# Chief Technology Officer (CTO) - Interview Questions

## A - Technology Vision & Strategy

### Open & Conversational

1. Please tell us about defining a multi-year technical vision across multiple business areas or divisions. How did you develop it?

2. Can you tell us about shaping enterprise architecture to support strategic goals? Walk us through your approach.

3. Tell us about evaluating an emerging technology and defining adoption timelines. What was the technology and what did you decide?

4. Can you tell us about balancing innovation with risk, cost, operational constraints, and long-term scalability? How do you manage these tensions?

5. Tell us about articulating a technology strategy that the board and executive team can execute against. What did you communicate?

6. Can you tell us about translating technology trends into business opportunities? Give us an example.

7. Tell us about making a strategic technology decision that positioned the company for future growth. What was the impact?

8. Can you tell us about aligning technology investments with market evolution and customer needs? How do you maintain alignment?

### Concise & Direct

1. Tell us about defining multi-year technical vision.

2. Tell us about shaping enterprise architecture.

3. Tell us about evaluating emerging technology.

4. Tell us about balancing innovation with constraints.

5. Tell us about articulating technology strategy to the board.

6. Tell us about translating tech trends to business opportunities.

7. Tell us about strategic technology decisions for growth.

8. Tell us about aligning technology with market needs.

### Guided & Specific

1. Please describe defining a multi-year technical vision across multiple business areas or divisions. What business context shaped your vision?

2. Can you walk us through demonstrating ability to shape enterprise architecture to support strategic goals? What architectural changes did you drive?

3. Please tell us about evaluating emerging technology, identifying strategic opportunities, and defining adoption timelines. What was your evaluation process?

4. Can you share an example of balancing innovation with risk, cost, operational constraints, and long-term scalability? How did you navigate the trade-offs?

5. Please describe articulating a technology strategy that the board and executive team can execute against. What made it actionable?

6. Can you tell us about translating technology trends into business opportunities and competitive advantages? What opportunity did you identify?

7. Please walk us through making strategic technology decisions that position the company for future growth. What was the strategic intent?

8. Can you describe aligning technology investments with market evolution and customer needs? How do you ensure ongoing relevance?

## Interview Styles

### Open & Conversational
Broad opening that lets the candidate choose their own example. Minimal scaffolding or direction - they pick the situation that matters to them.

*Best for:* Natural flow conversations, discovering what they naturally value and emphasise, seeing their own priorities.

### Concise & Direct
Minimal preamble, straight to the point. Questions are tight and assume the candidate understands what you're asking for.

*Best for:* Efficient interviews, fast-paced discussions, candidates with strong self-direction, interviewer who prefers not to scaffold.

### Guided & Specific
Points toward a particular situation type, but lets them choose their own example. Scaffolded enough to be clear what ground you want to cover.

*Best for:* Ensuring specific competencies are explored, guiding discussion while respecting their choice of example, structured assessment.
